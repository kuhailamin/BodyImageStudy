# Underweight and Overrepresented: Body Size Bias in AI-Generated Depictions of Women 
**Justin Thomas, Najud Al-Dugheithir, Fahad Al-Beyahi1, Mohammad Amin Kuhail**

The bias towards portraying thinner women in traditional media has been thoroughly demonstrated and well-documented. However, there has been limited systematic exploration of the extent to which this bias manifests in images generated by artificial intelligence (AI). This study examined viewers' perceptions of AI-generated depictions of women, assessing whether these images were seen as underweight, normal weight, or overweight. Using standardised prompts, images were generated depicting women in various occupations without specifying body shape or size (e.g., “draw a female lawyer”). The images were produced using three popular generative AI tools. Participants (N=129), adult women from the US, rated the body size of the AI-generated images using a shortened version of the Stunkard figure rating scale. Overall, the proportion of images perceived as underweight (the thinnest body size on the scale) was more than three times the population estimate for underweight among US women. Significant differences were also observed in body sizes generated by each AI platform. Across all platforms, higher occupational prestige was associated with more frequent perceptions of being underweight. These findings are discussed concerning AI bias and its possible implications for body image dissatisfaction and eating disorders.
